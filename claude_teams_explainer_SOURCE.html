Section 6 — Guardrails & Limits

What Claude Does Well — and Where It Falls Short

Claude is a capable but inherently imperfect tool. All LLMs can generate confident-sounding but factually wrong outputs. In October 2025, Deloitte submitted an AI-generated report to the Australian government containing fabricated sources. This is not a hypothetical risk. The guardrails below are non-negotiable.

✅

Claude Is Strong At

📝 First-draft narrative text and report sections
📊 Data table organization, formatting, formula writing
📧 Regulatory correspondence and cover letter drafting
🔍 Literature review and regulatory research synthesis
📑 Proposal, SOW, and template population
📋 Meeting notes, action items, admin writing
🗂️ Document extraction and summarization
📐 Cost estimate table structure and formatting
⛔

Never Rely on Claude For

❌ REC identification or Phase I professional judgment calls
❌ Regulatory threshold values — always provide the standard, don't ask Claude to recall it
❌ Final regulatory submissions without independent expert review
❌ Remedy selection or risk assessment decisions
❌ Interpretation requiring site reconnaissance or physical observation
❌ LSRP-signed documents without the LSRP's independent review
❌ Anything where you can't verify the output against a source document
The Operating Principle: Treat every Claude output as an unverified first draft from a capable but fallible junior employee. The qualified professional who signs or submits the work takes full responsibility for its accuracy — regardless of how it was generated.

Before Anything Else: Two Non-Negotiable Starting Points

These two deliverables should be developed in parallel with account setup — before any employee logs in for the first time. They are the foundation everything else is built on.

📋
AI Usage Policy

A well-designed policy doesn't just establish guardrails — it creates permission to explore. The goal is a document that is clear enough to protect the firm and flexible enough to encourage every employee to find their own best uses. Overly restrictive policies breed workarounds; overly vague ones breed liability. Get the balance right from the start.

What a Strong Policy Covers
▶ Data classification framework — Red/Yellow/Green tiers defining exactly what can and cannot go into Claude, written in plain language every employee can interpret without guessing
▶ Creative use encouragement — explicit language inviting employees to identify their own workflow inefficiencies and experiment with AI solutions, within the defined boundaries
▶ Human review mandate — clear requirement that all AI-assisted external deliverables are reviewed by a qualified professional before submission, full stop
▶ Approved tools only — Claude Teams via the firm account; no personal AI subscriptions used for firm business
▶ Living document cadence — quarterly review cycle built in, because AI capabilities are evolving faster than annual policy cycles can track
The policy should read like a tool for empowerment, not a list of things you can't do.
🎓
Company Training Materials

Impact Environmental's team spans a wide range of technical comfort levels — from employees who live in the terminal to those who prefer a phone call over email. Training materials need to meet every employee where they are, not where leadership wishes they were. One-size training produces half-adoption.

Two-Track Approach
🖥️ Tech-Comfortable Employees
Self-guided onboarding via Anthropic Academy courses (free, certified) · Role-specific prompt template library · Access to Claude Code documentation and GitHub tutorial · Encouraged to build and share custom Skills with the team · Monthly "what's working" share-out sessions
📞 Traditional Workflow Employees
Live walkthrough sessions with real Impact workflows — not abstract demos · Printed one-page quick-start guides per role (field tech, PM, admin) · Focused on two or three highest-value use cases per role, not the full feature set · Buddy system pairing with a tech-comfortable colleague · No pressure to master everything — start with one task, build from there
▶ Internal prompt library — a shared, firm-maintained collection of proven prompts for common tasks (Phase II data tables, proposal SOWs, NYSDEC letters) that any employee can copy and adapt immediately
▶ Measure adoption, not just access — track which tools and workflows are actually being used; non-use is data, and it points to where additional support is needed
The goal isn't compliance — it's genuine adoption. That requires meeting people where they are.
